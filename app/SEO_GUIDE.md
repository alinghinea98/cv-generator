# SEO Guide for makemecv.org

## What is SEO?

**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)** is the practice of improving your website's visibility in search engine results (Google, Bing, etc.). When someone searches for "CV builder" or "resume generator", you want your site to appear at the top.

## How SEO Works

1. **Crawling**: Search engines (like Google) send bots to visit your website
2. **Indexing**: They analyze your content and store it in their database
3. **Ranking**: When someone searches, Google shows the most relevant results first
4. **Factors**: Many factors affect ranking (content quality, speed, mobile-friendliness, backlinks, etc.)

- Tells search engines which pages to crawl
- Points to your sitemap
- Prevents indexing of private pages (login, wizard)

- Allows different meta tags for each page

## Next Steps to Improve SEO

### 1. **Google Search Console** (CRITICAL)
- Go to https://search.google.com/search-console
- Add your property: `makemecv.org`
- Verify ownership (DNS or HTML file)
- Submit your sitemap: `https://makemecv.org/sitemap.xml`
- Monitor how Google sees your site

### 2. **Google Analytics**
- Track visitors and behavior
- Understand what users search for to find you
- Install Google Analytics 4

### 3. **Content is King**
- Add a blog section with CV tips, resume advice
- Target keywords like:
  - "How to write a CV"
  - "CV template"
  - "Resume examples"
  - "ATS friendly CV"
- More content = more pages = more chances to rank

### 4. **Page Speed**
- Fast sites rank better
- Use Google PageSpeed Insights
- Optimize images
- Minimize JavaScript bundles

### 5. **Mobile-First**
- Most searches are on mobile
- Your site must be mobile-friendly
- Test on mobile devices

### 6. **Backlinks**
- Links from other sites to yours = trust
- Reach out to:
  - Job boards
  - Career advice blogs
  - University career centers
  - LinkedIn articles

### 7. **Local SEO** (if applicable)
- If targeting specific countries
- Add location-based keywords
- Create location pages

### 8. **User Experience Signals**
- Low bounce rate (users stay on site)
- High time on site
- Low exit rate
- These signal quality to Google
